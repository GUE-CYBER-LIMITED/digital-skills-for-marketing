## What are a Resume and Portfolio?

A **Resume** is like a **snapshot of your skills**, summarizing your education, experience, and abilities to impress employers. A **Portfolio** is a collection of your work (e.g., projects, designs) that shows what you can do, like a digital scrapbook.

Think of a resume and portfolio as a job interview outfit:
- The **resume** is your polished suit, giving a quick, professional overview.
- The **portfolio** is your accessories, showing your unique style (work samples).
- Together, they make you stand out in a crowd (job market).

This project uses Module 4's Google Docs for resumes and Module 5's communication for clarity.

### Why are a Resume and Portfolio Important?
Resumes and portfolios matter because they:
- **Get You Noticed**: Employers spend 6‚Äì8 seconds scanning resumes.
- **Showcase Skills**: Highlight abilities from Modules 1‚Äì6 (e.g., marketing, support).
- **Prove Experience**: Portfolios demonstrate real work, like campaign plans (Project 1).
- **Boost Confidence**: A strong resume makes you feel ready for opportunities.

## Key Concepts of a Resume and Portfolio

Let's explore the main components with examples for your Windows computer or Android phone.

### 1. Resume Structure
**What it is**: A resume has sections like contact info, summary, skills, experience, and education, written clearly (Module 5).

**Example**: On your Windows computer, you might write in Google Docs, "Skills: Digital Marketing, Excel, Customer Support." On your Android phone, you'd edit the same resume in the Docs app.

**Sections**:
- **Contact Info**: Name, email, phone.
- **Summary**: 2‚Äì3 sentences about you.
- **Skills**: List relevant abilities (e.g., SEO, Module 2).
- **Experience**: Jobs or projects (e.g., campaign plan, Project 1).
- **Education**: Degrees or courses.

**Illustration**: A resume is like a menu. It lists dishes (skills, experience) to entice the diner (employer).

### 2. Portfolio Content
**What it is**: A collection of work samples, like documents, screenshots, or links, showing your skills (e.g., Module 4's Excel charts).

**Example**: On your Android phone, you might save a Google Slides presentation to Google Drive as a portfolio piece. On your Windows computer, you'd add a link to it in a portfolio document.

**Illustration**: A portfolio is like an art gallery. You display paintings (projects) to wow visitors (employers).

### 3. Presentation and Sharing
**What it is**: Making your resume and portfolio look professional and easy to share, using tools like Google Drive (Module 4).

**Example**: On your Windows computer, you might save your resume as a PDF in Google Drive and share a link. On your Android phone, you'd email the link to a friend for feedback.

**Illustration**: Presentation is like gift-wrapping. A neat package (PDF, clean design) makes the gift (resume) exciting.

## How to Build a Resume and Portfolio

Here's how to create a resume and portfolio:

1. **Plan Your Resume**: List your skills, experience, and education (use Module 6 projects).  
   - Example: Include "Created marketing campaign plan" from Project 1.

2. **Write the Resume**: Use Google Docs to structure sections clearly (Module 5's clarity).  
   - Example: Write "Summary: Motivated learner skilled in marketing and support."

3. **Gather Portfolio Pieces**: Collect work samples (e.g., Project 1's plan, Project 2's case study).  
   - Example: Save a Google Sheet or campaign document.

4. **Organize the Portfolio**: Create a document or folder with links or files, explaining each piece.  
   - Example: List "Marketing Plan: Designed Instagram campaign" in Google Docs.

5. **Share Professionally**: Save as PDF, upload to Google Drive, and share via email (Module 5).  
   - Example: Email a resume and portfolio link to a pretend employer.

**Illustration**: Building a resume and portfolio is like cooking a meal:  
- **Plan**: Choose ingredients (skills).  
- **Write**: Cook the dish (resume).  
- **Gather**: Add sides (portfolio pieces).  
- **Organize**: Plate it nicely (portfolio document).  
- **Share**: Serve it (send to employer).

## Hands-On Activities

Let's build a resume and portfolio with fun activities on your **Windows computer** or **Android phone**. These are beginner-friendly and include comparisons to macOS, iOS, or Linux.

### Activity 1: Draft a Resume
**Goal**: Practice resume structure.  
**Steps (Windows)**:  
1. Open **Google Docs** or **Notepad** (Module 4).  
2. Write a short resume with 3 sections (e.g., Contact: Alex, alex@email.com; Skills: Excel, Marketing; Experience: Created campaign plan).  
3. Write down:  
   - The sections.  
   - Why it's professional (e.g., "Clear and concise").  
**Steps (Android)**:  
1. Open the **Google Docs** app or a note-taking app.  
2. Write a resume with 3 sections.  
3. Write down:  
   - The sections.  
   - Why it's professional.  
**Compare**: On iOS or Linux, Docs is similar. How might the interface differ? Write one difference.

**Example Answer (Windows)**:  
- Sections: Contact: Lisa, lisa@email.com; Skills: SEO, Support; Experience: Solved case study  
- Professional: Clear and concise, easy to read.  
- Difference: iOS Docs might have touch gestures.  

**Example Answer (Android)**:  
- Sections: Contact: Mike, mike@email.com; Skills: Excel, Emails; Experience: Marketing plan  
- Professional: Structured and professional tone.  
- Difference: Linux browsers might show simpler formatting.

### Activity 2: Plan a Portfolio
**Goal**: Practice collecting work samples.  
**Steps (Windows)**:  
1. Open **Google Docs** or **Notepad**.  
2. List 2 pretend portfolio pieces (e.g., "Marketing Plan: Instagram campaign, Support Case: Resolved billing issue").  
3. Write down:  
   - The pieces.  
   - How they show skills (e.g., "Marketing plan shows creativity").  
**Steps (Android)**:  
1. Open a note-taking app or **Google Docs** app.  
2. List 2 portfolio pieces.  
3. Write down:  
   - The pieces.  
   - How they show skills.  
**Compare**: On macOS or Linux, note-taking is similar. How might the app differ? Write one difference.

**Example Answer (Windows)**:  
- Pieces: Marketing Plan: Instagram campaign, Support Case: App fix  
- Skills: Marketing plan shows creativity, case shows problem-solving.  
- Difference: macOS Notes might sync with iCloud.  

**Example Answer (Android)**:  
- Pieces: Excel Budget: Monthly expenses, Email: Professional draft  
- Skills: Excel shows analysis, email shows communication.  
- Difference: Linux apps might be less touch-friendly.

### Activity 3: Share Your Work
**Goal**: Practice professional sharing.  
**Steps (Windows)**:  
1. In **Google Docs**, save your resume or portfolio list as a PDF (File > Download > PDF).  
2. Upload to **Google Drive** and create a shareable link.  
3. Write down:  
   - The shared item and link (or pretend link).  
   - Why it's professional (e.g., "PDF looks polished").  
**Steps (Android)**:  
1. In the **Google Docs** app, save as PDF and upload to **Google Drive**.  
2. Create a shareable link.  
3. Write down:  
   - The shared item and link.  
   - Why it's professional.  
**Compare**: On iOS or Linux, Drive is similar. How might sharing differ? Write one difference.

**Example Answer (Windows)**:  
- Item: Resume PDF, Link: [Pretend link]  
- Professional: PDF looks polished and easy to share.  
- Difference: iOS Drive might have smoother sharing options.  

**Example Answer (Android)**:  
- Item: Portfolio list PDF, Link: [Pretend link]  
- Professional: Clean format impresses employers.  
- Difference: Linux Drive might show larger icons.
